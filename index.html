A typical task that I face as an Inventory Specialist is balancing safety stock requirements. Below explains how my team and I make confident data driven safety stock adjustments.

JD Edwards

The first step to identifying safety stock requirements is retrieving the item usage data. I select a program and enter information for the query. I will then download the dataset as a CSV file.

Microsoft Excel

Next, I will open Excel and upload the CSV file. I use the Power Query Editor to the clean data. For example, I may have to split a column to create a new key. I also will remove columns, blanks, and errors. I will create formulas, filters, and functions. Then I will investigate specific areas and start to analyze the data.

Microsoft Power BI

The last step I take is to visualize and validate my findings with management. For example, I will make a line chart showing the increase/decrease usage of a specific item. This allows management and I to identify deficiencies and make data driven decisions to correct them.
